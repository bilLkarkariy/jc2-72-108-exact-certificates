And for n = 2, we have just moved the computational frontier.

In 2022, Guccione, Guccione, Horruitiner and Valqui showed that any planar counterexample with maximum degree below 125 must have degree pair (72,108), up to symmetry. One pair was left standing:

https://arxiv.org/abs/2204.14178

We have now excluded the two transcribed residual Laurent/Newton systems arising from their reduction by exact characteristic-zero certificate replay.

No floating-point approximations. No finite-field shortcut as the final argument.

The hard certificate is an 89.1 MB polynomial identity over a degree-5 number field. It was independently re-verified through 13,410 number-field products, equivalent to 335,250 exact scalar products.

Conditional on the correctness and exhaustiveness of the published reduction and our faithful transcription of it, every hypothetical counterexample to JC(2) must satisfy:

max(deg P, deg Q) >= 125.

This is not a proof of JC(2). It is a moved frontier.
